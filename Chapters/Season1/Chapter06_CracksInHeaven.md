Chapter 06 — Cracks in Heaven

Summary

Following Reika’s terrifying encounter with the presence behind the black star, the Tenshirō Clan is forced to confront a horrifying reality: the celestial seal protecting the world is deteriorating far faster than anyone predicted.

Across Tokyo, distortion zones continue expanding while reports of impossible phenomena multiply by the hour.

As Kairo searches for answers, Akari discovers a pattern hidden within the spreading distortions—one that suggests the fractures are not random but part of a deliberate celestial alignment.

Meanwhile, Reika struggles to hide the truth about the voice she heard inside the darkness.

And somewhere beneath the city, Zenith prepares the next phase of his plan.

Ending Cliffhanger

A visible fracture appears across the night sky above Tokyo—

and the entire world sees it.

Opening Scene

For three days—

Tokyo did not sleep.

News broadcasts ran twenty-four hours a day.

Emergency services remained overwhelmed.

And every night, more reports appeared.

Strange lights above the city.

People hearing voices where none existed.

Entire streets temporarily vanishing before reappearing hours later.

The government called them mass panic events.

No one believed that anymore.

The fear had become too visible.

Too real.

Rain clouds continued gathering above Tokyo, turning the skyline into a sea of gray steel and distant warning lights.

Inside the Tenshirō estate, the atmosphere had become increasingly tense.

The clan was no longer reacting.

It was preparing.

Ancient weapons were being removed from sealed vaults.

Barrier teams were deployed throughout the city.

And elders who had not left the estate grounds in years were suddenly attending emergency meetings.

Something was coming.

Everyone felt it.

Kairo stood alone in the training courtyard before sunrise.

His blade moved through the cold morning air in precise arcs.

Again.

Again.

Again.

But his thoughts were elsewhere.

The distortion.

The messenger.

The future fragments.

And most of all—

Reika.

She had not been the same since her collapse.

She smiled.

She talked.

She acted normal.

But Kairo knew her too well.

Something was wrong.

Very wrong.

Then the sky above the estate flickered.

Only for a second.

But Kairo saw it.

A thin silver line briefly appeared across the clouds overhead.

Like a crack spreading through glass.

Then it vanished.

His grip tightened around the blade.

Because deep down—

he already knew it wasn't a trick of the light.

Several hours later, the strategy chamber beneath the estate had become the busiest room in the entire clan compound.

Maps covered the walls.

Photographs of distortions filled large tables.

Reports arrived faster than analysts could process them.

At the center of the chaos—

Akari stared silently at a massive map of Japan.

Something was wrong.

Very wrong.

She could feel it.

Threads of pale blue light drifted around her fingers as she connected locations one by one.

Shinjuku.

The eastern subway sector.

Three coastal anomalies.

Two mountain disturbances.

And dozens of smaller celestial incidents reported during the previous seventy-two hours.

At first glance, the events appeared random.

But they weren't.

Akari's eyes widened suddenly.

“No...”

Several nearby analysts looked toward her immediately.

“What is it?”

She ignored them.

Hands moving rapidly across the map.

Connecting point after point.

Drawing invisible lines only she could see.

Then she stepped backward.

The room fell silent.

Because the pattern was obvious now.

Terrifyingly obvious.

Every distortion site formed part of a massive geometric structure stretching across the country.

A symbol.

Ancient.

Celestial.

Forbidden.

Akari felt her stomach drop.

She recognized it.

Not from history books.

From visions.

From dreams she had never understood.

Kairo entered the chamber moments later.

One look at Akari's face told him everything.

Something had gone horribly wrong.

“What happened?”

Akari slowly pointed toward the completed pattern.

Her voice barely rose above a whisper.

“…They're not opening distortions.”

Silence.

Then:

“They're drawing a seal.”

The room froze.

Because if the distortions were creating a seal—

then the obvious question became:

What exactly was it meant to contain?

The strategy chamber remained silent.

No one moved.

No one spoke.

Every eye remained fixed on the symbol spread across the map.

Kairo looked toward Kaze immediately.

Because only one person in the room looked more disturbed than Akari.

The patriarch.

For several seconds, Kaze simply stared.

Then he closed his eyes.

A rare gesture.

One that instantly raised the tension inside the room.

“…Impossible.”

Akari swallowed.

“You recognize it.”

It was not a question.

Kaze slowly opened his eyes again.

“The Outer Seal.”

The words meant nothing to most of the room.

But the few elder clan members present visibly stiffened.

One of them actually stepped backward.

Fear crossing his face.

Kairo noticed immediately.

“What is the Outer Seal?”

Kaze remained silent for a long moment.

Then finally answered.

“Before the Celestial Eyes existed…”

The room grew still.

“…there were other methods.”

Akari's heart sank.

Ancient methods.

The kind that predated modern history.

The kind usually buried for good reasons.

Kaze pointed toward the pattern.

“That symbol was designed to imprison things that could not be destroyed.”

The room felt colder suddenly.

Kairo looked back toward the map.

Toward the enormous structure spreading across Japan.

Then a realization struck him.

“If they're drawing a seal…”

His voice lowered.

“…then something has already escaped.”

No one answered.

Because everyone had just reached the same conclusion.

And none of them wanted to say it aloud.

Far away—

thunder rolled across the sky above Tokyo.

And for a brief moment, the clouds seemed to pulse with faint silver light hidden behind them.

That night—

the sky changed.

Not dramatically.

Not at first.

Most people never noticed it.

A faint silver line appeared high above Tokyo shortly after sunset.

Thin.

Almost invisible.

Like a scratch across glass.

Meteorologists dismissed it as an unusual atmospheric effect.

Astronomers were less certain.

Within hours, observatories across Japan began reporting irregular readings.

The stars themselves appeared slightly displaced.

Not enough for ordinary people to notice.

Enough for experts to panic.

Inside the Tenshirō estate, Akari stood alone on the highest observation platform.

Her threads drifted nervously around her hands.

The future felt wrong.

Every pathway she followed eventually disappeared into a blank horizon she could not see beyond.

That had never happened before.

Then she looked up.

And froze.

The silver line was growing.

Slowly.

Almost imperceptibly.

But it was growing.

Akari's breathing became uneven.

“No...”

The threads around her fingers snapped violently.

One after another.

The sound echoed through the silence.

Far below, Kairo stepped onto the platform after receiving her emergency message.

The moment he saw her face, he knew something was wrong.

“What happened?”

Akari pointed upward.

Kairo followed her gaze.

The moment he saw the silver fracture—

the Heavenly Eye activated on its own.

Pain exploded behind his vision.

Future fragments flooded his mind.

He saw:

A sky split into countless pieces.

Cities illuminated by celestial fire.

Millions staring upward in terror.

And beyond the fracture—

an endless darkness filled with distant stars.

Then the vision ended.

Kairo staggered backward.

Blood dripped from beneath his eye.

Akari stared at him.

“What did you see?”

Kairo looked up at the growing crack.

His voice barely rose above a whisper.

“…The sky opens.”

Far above them—

the silver fracture widened another fraction of an inch.

Reika had stopped sleeping.

At least—

truly sleeping.

Every time she closed her eyes, the darkness returned.

The black star.

The endless void.

The voice.

Always waiting.

Always watching.

She sat quietly inside the estate library long after midnight, surrounded by ancient books she wasn't actually reading.

The pages blurred together.

Her thoughts never did.

The golden cracks beneath her skin had spread farther again.

She kept them hidden beneath long sleeves.

For now.

A soft sound came from the doorway.

Kairo.

Of course.

Reika almost smiled.

Almost.

“You should be asleep.”

Kairo leaned against the doorframe.

“So should you.”

Neither believed the other.

For a moment, silence settled between them.

Comfortable.

Familiar.

Then Kairo spoke.

“You haven't been okay since the collapse.”

The words landed harder than he intended.

Because they were true.

Reika looked down.

“You make it sound like that's new.”

Kairo didn't smile.

That worried her immediately.

He stepped farther into the room.

“Something happened.”

Not a question.

A statement.

A brother's certainty.

Reika stared at the table.

At her hands.

At anything except him.

Because if she looked directly at Kairo—

she might tell him everything.

The voice.

The star.

The thing waiting beyond the darkness.

Instead she chose a smaller truth.

The safest truth.

“I hear things sometimes.”

Silence.

Kairo's expression changed instantly.

Not fear.

Concern.

“What kind of things?”

Reika swallowed.

Her throat suddenly felt dry.

“Whispers.”

The word barely escaped her lips.

The library seemed colder afterward.

Kairo remained silent.

Listening.

Waiting.

Reika forced herself to continue.

“Sometimes it's just my name.”

She laughed weakly.

A broken sound.

“Sometimes it feels like something is trying to remember me.”

The moment the words left her mouth—

the candles throughout the library flickered.

Every flame bent in the same direction.

Toward her.

Neither twin spoke.

Because deep down—

both of them knew that wasn't normal.

Far beneath the city—

the Veilbreakers were celebrating.

Not loudly.

Not recklessly.

With certainty.

The hidden chamber beneath Tokyo pulsed with faint black light as more fractures spread across the ancient seal embedded into the earth.

Zenith stood before it in silence.

Watching.

Waiting.

Listening.

One of the Veilbreakers approached carefully.

“The celestial fracture has appeared.”

Zenith nodded.

“The first one.”

“The clan has noticed.”

A faint smile touched his face.

“They were always going to notice.”

The Veilbreaker hesitated.

Then asked the question none of them truly understood.

“Why now?”

The chamber fell silent.

Zenith looked toward the fractured seal.

Toward the darkness beyond it.

For a long moment he said nothing.

Then—

“Because the prison is waking up.”

The air itself seemed to grow heavier.

Even the Veilbreakers stepped back instinctively.

Zenith's fractured Abyss Eye opened slowly.

Black light spread through the chamber.

“History remembers the Celestial Eyes as blessings.”

His voice remained calm.

Measured.

“But history was written by the jailers.”

The seal pulsed.

A deep sound echoed from somewhere beyond reality.

Like a heartbeat.

Ancient.

Impossible.

Zenith lowered his gaze.

“For the first time in centuries…”

The black fractures spread farther.

“…the prisoner remembers its name.”

Far above them—

the silver crack in the sky widened again.

The fracture appeared at 11:43 PM.

This time—

everyone saw it.

At first, people thought it was lightning.

A thin silver line spreading across the night sky above Tokyo.

Then it kept growing.

And growing.

And growing.

News helicopters captured it live.

Millions watched from their homes.

Millions more stood outside staring upward.

The line stretched across the heavens like a crack spreading through glass.

Phones recorded it.

Telescopes tracked it.

Scientists panicked.

Nobody could explain it.

Inside the Tenshirō estate, every alarm activated simultaneously.

Clan members rushed through the halls.

Barrier systems flared to life.

Ancient seals hidden beneath the estate began glowing for the first time in decades.

Kairo and Reika emerged onto the highest observation platform together.

The moment they saw the sky—

both froze.

The fracture now stretched from horizon to horizon.

Silver light poured through it.

Not outward.

Inward.

As though something beyond reality was looking through the opening.

Akari arrived moments later, breathing heavily.

Her future threads had become completely unstable.

“I can't see past tomorrow.”

Silence.

That statement terrified everyone present.

Because Akari had always been able to see farther than that.

Always.

Then—

the fracture moved.

Not widened.

Moved.

Like a living thing.

A ripple passed across the entire crack.

The sky shuddered.

And for one brief moment—

every person looking upward saw the same thing.

An enormous shadow passing behind the fracture.

Watching.

The vision lasted less than a second.

Then it vanished.

But the damage was done.

Because now the entire world knew:

Something existed on the other side.

The world did not sleep that night.

Every news channel replayed the footage endlessly.

Every government agency entered emergency session.

Every observatory on Earth pointed its instruments toward the fracture.

And none of them found answers.

Only more questions.

Inside the Tenshirō estate, the atmosphere had become suffocating.

No one spoke above a whisper.

No one pretended things were normal anymore.

Because normal had ended.

Kairo stood alone on the observation platform long after everyone else had left.

The silver fracture still stretched across the heavens.

Silent.

Watching.

Waiting.

Then he heard footsteps.

Reika.

She stopped beside him.

For a while neither twin spoke.

The city lights flickered beneath them.

The fractured sky loomed above them.

Then Reika quietly asked:

“Do you think it saw us?”

Kairo looked upward.

The question sounded ridiculous.

Impossible.

And yet—

after what they had witnessed—

he could not honestly say no.

Before he could answer—

pain exploded through Reika's body.

She collapsed instantly.

Golden light erupted beneath her skin.

The cracks spread violently across her arms and neck.

Kairo caught her before she hit the ground.

“Reika!”

The sky responded.

The silver fracture pulsed.

Once.

Twice.

Then a third time.

As though reacting to her.

Reika's eyes opened.

But for a brief moment—

they were not her eyes.

A vast black star reflected within them.

Ancient.

Endless.

Hungry.

And a voice echoed across her mind.

Clearer than ever before.

«“The gate remembers.”»

The world seemed to stop.

Then came the second message.

One that chilled her more than anything before.

«“The others are coming.”»

The vision ended instantly.

The golden light vanished.

The cracks stopped spreading.

And Reika fell unconscious in Kairo's arms.

Far above them—

the fracture widened.

Just a little more.
