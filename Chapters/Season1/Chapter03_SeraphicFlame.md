the Seraphic Eye fully opens.

Opening Scene

Tokyo was changing.

Not slowly.

Not quietly.

Entire sections of the eastern district flickered with unnatural distortions as reality bent beneath the blood moon overhead. Streetlights warped. Shadows moved incorrectly. Buildings shimmered for brief moments like broken reflections trapped underwater.

And people were beginning to notice.

Phones across the city flooded with videos of impossible phenomena.

Black rain.
Floating ash.
Whispers without owners.

Panic spread faster than the authorities could suppress it.

Inside the Tenshirō estate, the atmosphere had descended into controlled chaos.

Clan members rushed through the halls carrying weapons, barrier seals, and ancient ocular artifacts long untouched beneath the estate vaults.

The emergency protocols had been activated.

Which meant only one thing.

The situation had surpassed containment.

Kairo stood near the estate entrance fastening the dark combat uniform worn only by active members of the bloodline.

The moment he tightened the final strap around his wrist—

pain pulsed sharply behind his left eye again.

A fragment of another vision flashed violently through his mind.

Fire.

Screaming.

Golden wings burning beneath the night sky.

Then darkness.

Kairo exhaled slowly.

The Heavenly Eye was becoming harder to control.

Behind him, footsteps approached carefully.

Reika.

For a moment, neither twin spoke.

She wore the same black combat attire as him, though faint traces of golden light still flickered beneath the bandages wrapped around her arms.

Kairo immediately noticed the fresh bindings.

“…It spread again.”

Reika looked away.

Only slightly.

“That’s not important right now.”

“It is to me.”

Silence.

Then suddenly—

every alarm inside the estate activated at once.

A clan operator’s voice echoed across the estate through the emergency communication system.

“Reality distortion confirmed across multiple eastern sectors.”

“Civilian casualties rising.”

“Barrier collapse detected near Shinjuku District.”

Another voice immediately followed.

“Corrupted ocular signatures identified.”

The hall went silent for half a second.

Even among the Tenshirō Clan, those words carried fear.

Corrupted users were rare.

And almost never survived long enough to spread.

Kaze entered the corridor moments later surrounded by several senior clan members.

His presence alone stabilized the atmosphere slightly.

“The Veilbreakers are forcing unstable awakenings,” he said coldly.

“They’re accelerating resonance.”

Akari stepped forward immediately.

“That’s impossible. The human mind can’t withstand forced synchronization.”

Kaze’s expression darkened.

“They don’t intend for the hosts to survive.”

A heavy silence followed.

Kairo’s jaw tightened.

Outside the estate, distant explosions suddenly echoed through Tokyo.

The city skyline flickered beneath the blood moon.

Smoke rising into the heavens.

Reika’s breathing became uneven again.

The Seraphic Eye was reacting violently.

Kairo noticed her fingers trembling slightly.

“You’re staying here.”

Her head snapped toward him instantly.

“No.”

“You can barely control the resonance.”

“And you think I’ll let you go alone?”

Golden light flickered briefly through her crimson eye.

For a split second—

the pressure in the hallway intensified enough to crack the nearby walls.

Several younger clan members immediately stepped back in fear.

Reika noticed.

And hated it.

The fear in their eyes.

The silence after her power surfaced.

Like everyone was waiting for her to become something monstrous.

Kairo stepped closer quietly.

“…Reika.”

Her expression weakened immediately.

Just hearing her name from him always did that.

Then—

another distortion pulse hit Tokyo.

This time stronger.

And somewhere deep within the city—

something screamed.

Shinjuku looked like a city trapped between realities.

By the time Kairo and Reika arrived, entire sections of the district had already been evacuated. Police barricades twisted unnaturally beneath invisible pressure while abandoned vehicles sat frozen across intersections under the crimson glow of the blood moon.

The air itself felt wrong.

Heavy.

Distorted.

Fragments of black ash drifted slowly downward like snow.

Several emergency responders stood motionless near the center of the street.

Alive.

But empty.

Their eyes had turned completely black.

Kairo stopped immediately.

“…Don’t get close.”

The Heavenly Eye pulsed painfully again.

Future fragments scattered violently through his vision.

Death routes.

Impact trajectories.

Possible outcomes.

Too many.

A low growling sound echoed from deeper within the ruined street ahead.

Then figures emerged from the smoke.

Humans.

At least they used to be.

Dark fractures spread beneath their skin while unstable ocular markings burned across their faces like corrupted symbols carved directly into flesh.

Their movements were unnatural.

Bodies jerking violently between moments of stillness.

One of them suddenly looked directly at Reika.

And screamed.

The sound distorted the air itself.

Every corrupted user immediately charged at once.

Kairo moved instantly.

“LEFT—”

Reika reacted without hesitation.

Golden flame exploded outward from her arm in a massive arc, vaporizing the nearest attacker before impact.

The street shattered beneath the force.

Heat flooded the district instantly.

Nearby windows exploded outward.

The remaining corrupted users continued rushing forward despite their bodies literally burning apart.

Kairo’s eye activated.

The world slowed.

He saw:

- trajectories
- fractures
- movement patterns
- future impacts

Everything became lines.

One step forward.

One rotation.

One strike.

Kairo intercepted the next attacker with brutal precision, driving his blade directly through the corrupted ocular core embedded within its chest.

Black blood splattered across the pavement.

But more were coming.

Far more.

And above them—

the blood moon continued watching silently.

More corrupted users emerged from the smoke.

Too many.

Their distorted screams echoed across the ruined streets as black veins pulsed beneath their skin like living parasites.

Kairo immediately understood the problem.

They were not fighting strategically.

They were being drawn somewhere.

Toward Reika.

One of the creatures lunged directly toward her with impossible speed.

Kairo moved to intercept—

too late.

The corrupted user reached her.

Its hand almost touched her face.

Then—

silence.

Golden light erupted across the entire district.

The world stopped moving for half a second.

A massive ring of celestial flame exploded outward from Reika’s body, swallowing the street in blinding gold-white fire.

Every corrupted user caught within the blast disintegrated instantly.

Not burned.

Erased.

The buildings surrounding the impact zone trembled violently beneath the pressure.

Massive wings of radiant flame manifested behind Reika for a brief moment—

beautiful and terrifying.

Kairo shielded his eyes instinctively.

Even he could barely look directly at the light.

Reika stood motionless at the center of the destroyed street.

Her crimson eye had changed completely.

A glowing golden sigil now rotated slowly within it like a divine mechanism awakening.

The Seraphic Eye.

Fully active.

But something was wrong.

Very wrong.

Golden cracks spread rapidly across Reika’s neck and arms as the flames around her intensified uncontrollably.

She looked horrified.

“Kairo…”

Her voice shook.

The fire behind her surged violently again.

“I can’t stop it.”

Far above the city—

every cloud surrounding the blood moon split apart at once.

And across Tokyo—

millions looked up at the sky.
