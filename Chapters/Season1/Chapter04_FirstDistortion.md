Summary

Opening Scene

By morning—

Tokyo already knew.

News stations flooded every broadcast channel with distorted footage captured throughout Shinjuku during the previous night. Videos of golden celestial flames spread uncontrollably across the internet despite government suppression efforts.

Some called it terrorism.

Others called it mass hallucination.

But millions had seen the sky split open with their own eyes.

And fear spread faster than truth ever could.

Rain fell steadily across the city as emergency sirens echoed endlessly through the streets below towering digital billboards now displaying evacuation warnings across multiple eastern districts.

People no longer looked at the sky comfortably.

Not after last night.

Inside the upper levels of the Tenshirō estate, silence dominated the corridors.

The atmosphere felt heavy.

Ashamed.

Several clan members openly avoided speaking Reika’s name entirely.

As if acknowledging what happened in Shinjuku would somehow make it more real.

Kairo stood alone near one of the estate windows overlooking the rain-covered city skyline.

He hadn’t slept.

Every time he closed his eyes—

he saw those future fragments again.

Tokyo burning beneath black stars.

Reika consumed by celestial fire.

The end of something.

Or perhaps the beginning.

Behind him, the door opened quietly.

“Kairo.”

Akari entered holding several unfolded reports covered in celestial markings and handwritten calculations.

Her expression looked pale.

Worried.

“That wasn’t the only incident last night.”

Kairo turned immediately.

Akari placed the reports across the table.

Photographs.
Distorted streets.
Collapsed buildings.
People reporting impossible sounds beneath the city.

And then—

one image froze him completely.

A narrow alleyway somewhere in eastern Tokyo.

The walls had bent inward unnaturally.

Like reality itself had folded.

At the center of the distortion—

the shadow of a massive eye could be seen staring outward from the darkness.

Akari spread additional photographs across the table.

Every single image showed impossible geometry.

Buildings folding inward.
Streets repeating themselves.
Shadows moving in directions opposite to light.

And always—

the eye.

Watching from somewhere inside the distortions.

“These incidents started less than three hours after the Shinjuku event,” Akari explained quietly.

“The resonance pulse from Reika’s flames accelerated something.”

Kairo’s expression hardened.

“So this is our fault.”

“No.”

A new voice entered the room.

Kaze.

The patriarch stepped inside slowly, rainwater still dripping faintly from the edges of his dark robes.

“This was already coming.”

The room fell silent.

Kairo immediately noticed something unusual.

Kaze looked exhausted.

Not physically.

Emotionally.

As though he had been carrying knowledge he no longer knew how to contain.

“The distortions are forming around weakened celestial boundaries,” Kaze continued.

“Places where reality was already unstable long before any of you were born.”

Akari’s eyes widened slightly.

“…Ancient fracture points.”

Kaze nodded once.

“The First Crater was never the only place Mikaboshi touched this world.”

A cold silence followed.

Then suddenly—

all the lights inside the room flickered violently.

The temperature dropped instantly.

Akari grabbed the edge of the table sharply.

“The threads just changed.”

Kairo stepped forward immediately.

“What happened?”

Her voice shook.

“One of the distortions stabilized.”

That sentence froze the room.

Because unstable distortions collapsed naturally.

Always.

If one had stabilized—

then something inside it was anchoring reality from the other side.

Kaze’s expression darkened instantly.

“Location?”

Akari looked down at the trembling threads surrounding her fingers.

“…Eastern subway sector.”

Silence.

Then Kairo immediately turned toward the door.

“I’m going.”

Kaze did not stop him this time.

Because both of them already understood.

Whatever had appeared inside Tokyo—

was no longer simply a distortion.

The eastern subway sector had already been abandoned by the time Kairo arrived.

Rainwater dripped steadily through cracked concrete ceilings as emergency lights flickered weakly across the empty underground station.

No trains moved anymore.

No voices echoed through the tunnels.

Only silence.

And something beneath it.

Kairo stepped carefully across the platform, his blade resting loosely at his side while the Heavenly Eye pulsed faintly behind his vision.

The distortion was close.

He could feel it.

The deeper he walked into the station—

the less normal the environment became.

Signs along the walls repeated themselves endlessly.

The same advertisement appeared seven times in a row despite the tunnel continuing forward.

Even the clock above the platform moved incorrectly.

Its hands rotated backward.

Kairo stopped.

The Heavenly Eye activated instinctively.

Future fragments scattered through his vision—

and every single one ended in darkness.

That had never happened before.

A low sound echoed somewhere deeper underground.

Not footsteps.

Breathing.

Slow.

Massive.

Kairo tightened his grip on the blade.

Then he finally saw it.

At the far end of the tunnel—

space itself had opened.

The distortion hung suspended in the darkness like a tear carved directly into reality.

Black fragments floated weightlessly around its edges while faint whispers echoed from somewhere inside.

And at its center—

an enormous eye stared back at him.

Not human.

Not celestial.

Ancient.

Its pupil shifted slowly toward Kairo.

Then—

it blinked.

The entire subway tunnel distorted violently.

For a brief moment—

Kairo saw another world behind the tear.

Black skies.

Endless stars.

And towering shapes moving beneath them.

The vision vanished instantly.

But the fear remained.

Because something inside the distortion had just seen him too.

The eye inside the distortion continued staring at him.

Unmoving.

Unblinking.

The pressure flooding the subway tunnel intensified with every passing second until even breathing began feeling unnatural.

Kairo took a slow step backward.

The distortion reacted instantly.

The black tear across reality expanded violently.

Concrete walls twisted inward.
Lights exploded overhead.
The tunnel screamed.

Then something emerged.

A hand.

Long.

Pale.

Inhuman fingers clawed slowly through the fracture in space before gripping the edge of the distortion like a living wound.

Kairo’s instincts activated immediately.

MOVE.

The Heavenly Eye exploded into motion.

Time fractured around him.

Future pathways unfolded simultaneously through his vision:

- death by impact
- death by collapse
- death by direct contact

Too many.

Something inside the distortion lunged outward.

Kairo barely dodged as a massive black limb tore through the tunnel behind him, carving directly through concrete and steel like paper.

The subway station collapsed violently.

Dust and debris filled the air.

Kairo landed hard across the ruined platform, blood running slowly from beneath his left eye.

The Heavenly Eye was overheating.

Again.

The distortion pulsed violently behind the creature still forcing itself into reality.

Its body did not fully obey physical law.

Limbs stretched incorrectly.
Its shape changed every time Kairo looked directly at it.

Like reality itself could not decide what it was supposed to be.

Then the creature spoke.

Not aloud.

Inside his mind.

«“The gate… opens…”»

Kairo froze.

The voice felt ancient beyond comprehension.

Hungry.

Lonely.

The creature moved again.

Faster this time.

Kairo activated the Heavenly Eye completely.

The world stopped.

And for the first time—

he forced himself to look directly into the future.

Silence consumed the world.

Everything stopped.

Dust hung motionless in the air.
Broken concrete floated mid-collapse.
Even the creature emerging from the distortion froze beneath the influence of the Heavenly Eye.

Only Kairo could move.

Pain exploded behind his left eye as countless future pathways unfolded around him simultaneously like shattered glass suspended across reality.

Thousands of outcomes.

Thousands of deaths.

But buried among them—

one surviving path.

Kairo saw it instantly.

Three seconds forward.

One movement left.

Blade rotation at thirty-two degrees.

Impact directly through the unstable resonance core forming inside the creature’s chest.

The Heavenly Eye burned brighter.

Blood ran heavily from beneath Kairo’s eye now.

His body was reaching its limit.

But he moved anyway.

Time resumed violently.

Kairo accelerated forward with explosive force, narrowly avoiding the creature’s distorted limbs as reality warped around every movement it made.

The creature screamed inside his mind.

«“CHILD OF THE FALSE STARS—”»

Kairo drove his blade forward.

Directly into the glowing fracture hidden within the creature’s chest.

The distortion collapsed instantly.

Black light erupted across the subway tunnel as the entity convulsed violently before disintegrating into countless floating fragments of shadow.

Then silence returned.

The creature was gone.

But the distortion remained.

Smaller now.

Unstable.

Breathing slowly like a wounded living thing.

Kairo staggered backward immediately.

His vision blurred violently.

The Heavenly Eye deactivated.

And for a brief moment—

he saw another future fragment.

Not Tokyo.

Not Reika.

Himself.

Standing alone beneath an endless black sky.

Blind.

The vision disappeared instantly.

Kairo’s breathing became uneven.

“…What did I just kill?”

Then from deep inside the distortion—

something answered him.

«“A messenger.”»
