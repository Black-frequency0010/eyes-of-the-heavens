The night begins revealing truths the clan was never meant to face.

Opening Scene

The wind outside the estate had changed.

It no longer felt natural.

The trees surrounding the Tenshirō grounds bent slowly despite the absence of rain, their shadows stretching unnaturally across the stone pathways beneath the crimson moonlight.

Violet stood motionless near the outer gate.

Calm.

One hand resting loosely inside the pocket of his black coat as shattered violet light flickered faintly through the cracks surrounding his right eye.

He looked less like a human intruder—

and more like a distortion wearing human skin.

No one approached him.

Not even the armed clan guards stationed along the walls.

Fear held them in place.

Inside the main hall, Kairo continued staring toward the entrance in silence.

That vision still lingered in his mind.

Tokyo burning.

Black stars falling from the sky.

Screams swallowed by darkness.

And at the center of it all—

Reika.

“Kairo.”

Akari’s voice pulled him back slightly.

“The future threads around him are wrong.”

He glanced toward her.

“What does that mean?”

Akari’s pale blue threads trembled violently around her fingertips.

“…I can’t see where he ends.”

A quiet unease spread through the hall.

Kaze stepped forward at last.

“Open the inner path.”

Several clan members immediately looked shocked.

“Lord Kaze—?”

“He came here alone,” Kaze said calmly.

“That means this is not an attack.”

Violet smiled faintly outside the gate.

“Still sharp as ever.”

The massive doors of the estate slowly began opening.

And for the first time in years—

a Veilbreaker walked willingly into the heart of the Tenshirō Clan.

The main hall remained completely silent as Violet entered.

Every step echoed softly against the polished black floor beneath the crimson light filtering through the windows.

Several clan guards instinctively tightened their grip on their weapons.

Violet noticed.

He laughed quietly.

“Relax.”

His fractured eye glowed faintly.

“If I wanted this place destroyed, we wouldn’t be having a conversation.”

Kairo stepped forward immediately.

Positioning himself slightly in front of Reika.

Violet’s smile widened almost imperceptibly.

“There it is.”

Kairo’s expression remained unreadable.

“What do you want?”

“A question.”

“That’s all?”

“For now.”

The atmosphere tightened further.

Violet slowly looked around the hall.

At the ancient symbols carved into the walls.
At the celestial markings surrounding the pillars.
At the clan members standing frozen beneath generations of inherited fear.

Then finally—

he looked toward Kaze.

“Tell me something, Patriarch.”

His voice softened.

“When was the last time your family looked at the stars… without fear?”

No one answered.

Not immediately.

Because somewhere deep inside themselves—

they all understood the truth behind the question.

Violet turned slightly toward Kairo.

“You think your eyes were chosen by heaven.”

His fractured eye pulsed once.

“But what if heaven was never on your side?”

The pressure in the room shifted instantly.

Reika suddenly grabbed her arm sharply.

Golden cracks spread briefly beneath her skin again.

Kairo noticed Violet watching her carefully.

Not with hatred.

Not even hostility.

Pity.

That disturbed him more than anything else.

“You’re lying,” one of the clan guards snapped suddenly.

Violet barely looked toward him.

“No.”

The man’s body froze instantly.

Invisible pressure crushed the air around him.

Not enough to kill.

Just enough to silence.

Kairo’s eyes narrowed immediately.

No visible activation.
No hand signs.
No movement.

Yet Violet had bent reality itself for a split second.

“…Fractured Eye,” Kairo murmured internally.

Dangerous.
Far more dangerous than the reports claimed.

The pressure inside the hall continued rising.

Not violently.

Slowly.

Like invisible hands tightening around everyone’s throat.

Reika’s breathing became uneven.

The golden cracks beneath her skin spread slightly farther up her arm before fading again.

Violet noticed immediately.

“…So it’s already progressing.”

Kairo stepped forward sharply.

“What does that mean?”

For the first time since entering the estate—

Violet’s expression lost its amusement.

“The seal is weakening faster than expected.”

Kaze’s voice cut through the hall instantly.

“Enough.”

The temperature in the room dropped.

Tiny particles of starlike light began drifting around the patriarch once more.

Violet looked toward him calmly.

“You know I’m right.”

“Leave.”

“You felt it too tonight.”

Silence.

The blood moonlight outside deepened unnaturally.

Violet’s fractured eye reflected against the polished floor like broken glass.

“The stars stopped moving.”

That sentence froze the entire room.

Even Kaze said nothing.

Violet slowly turned toward Kairo again.

“You saw something, didn’t you?”

The Heavenly Eye pulsed painfully.

A sharp distortion ripped through Kairo’s vision.

For a brief instant—

he saw countless black hands reaching down from the sky above Tokyo.

Then—

Reika screaming.

Kairo staggered slightly.

Reika immediately grabbed his arm.

“Kairo!”

The vision disappeared.

His breathing had become uneven.

Violet watched silently.

“…It’s starting earlier this time.”

Kairo looked up sharply.

“…This time?”

Violet smiled again.

But this time—

there was no warmth in it at all.

Then suddenly—

every light inside the estate exploded at once.

Darkness swallowed the hall.

And from somewhere far beneath Tokyo—

something laughed.

Emergency lights flickered dimly across the hall.

Red.

Unstable.

The atmosphere no longer felt like the Tenshirō estate.

It felt like something else had begun overlapping with reality itself.

Several younger clan members had already collapsed under the pressure.

Others stared toward the floor in silence, trying not to panic.

Only Violet remained completely calm.

He looked almost disappointed.

“…So it really has begun.”

Kairo forced himself upright despite the lingering pain behind his eyes.

“What is happening beneath Tokyo?”

Violet looked toward him quietly.

Then—

“The cage is cracking.”

Silence.

Reika’s fingers tightened around Kairo’s sleeve unconsciously.

Kaze’s expression darkened.

“You should not have come here.”

“No,” Violet replied softly.

“You should be grateful that I did.”

The fractured eye glowed again.

“Because tonight was never supposed to start with me.”

A heavy silence fell over the hall.

Then Akari suddenly froze.

Her eyes widened in absolute terror.

“The threads…”

Blood slowly began running from her nose.

“They’re disappearing across the entire eastern district.”

Kairo’s heartbeat slowed instantly.

Not from calm.

From instinct.

Something catastrophic was approaching.

Violet turned toward the shattered windows.

Toward Tokyo.

Toward the distant skyline beneath the blood-red heavens.

Then he spoke one final sentence.

“…Zenith has entered the city.”

For the first time that night—

fear visibly crossed Kaze Tenshirō’s face.

And somewhere far beyond the estate—

the sound of church bells echoed across Tokyo at midnight.
