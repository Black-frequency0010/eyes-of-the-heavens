Chapter 05 — The Watcher In Black 

Summary

After surviving the first stabilized distortion zone, Kairo returns to the Tenshirō estate carrying disturbing revelations about the entities emerging from beyond reality.

As Tokyo descends further into fear and instability, the clan realizes the distortions are not random phenomena—but deliberate fractures connected to Ama-no-Mikaboshi’s awakening.

Meanwhile, Reika struggles with growing terror toward her own Seraphic Eye after its public manifestation beneath the blood moon.

As tensions rise within the clan, Kaze finally reveals part of the hidden truth behind the Celestial Eyes, the Silent Heaven, and the ancient pact that created the bloodlines themselves.

And for the first time—

the words “Trinity Resonance” are spoken openly.

Opening Scene

Rain continued falling across Tokyo.

But now the city felt different.

Quieter.

As if the entire world had become afraid of making noise.

Emergency broadcasts looped endlessly across television screens while rumors of “black-eyed people” and “living distortions” spread uncontrollably through the internet.

The government denied everything.

No one believed them anymore.

Inside the Tenshirō estate, the atmosphere had become suffocating.

Clan members spoke in whispers.
Hallways remained half-empty.
Even the servants avoided eye contact.

Fear had entered the estate.

And fear spread quickly inside ancient houses built upon secrets.

Kairo sat alone inside one of the lower medical chambers beneath the estate while healers worked carefully around the damage surrounding his left eye.

The Heavenly Eye had pushed far beyond safe limits during the distortion encounter.

Thin veins of silver light still flickered faintly beneath the skin around his eye socket.

“You should be resting,” Akari said quietly from nearby.

Kairo remained staring toward the floor.

“It spoke to me.”

The room became still.

Akari slowly lowered the reports she had been reading.

“…The entity?”

Kairo nodded once.

“It called itself a messenger.”

Silence followed.

Rain echoed softly against the estate windows far above them.

Then Kairo finally looked up.

“…What if the distortions aren’t random?”

Akari’s expression tightened immediately.

“You think they’re intentional.”

“I think something on the other side is searching.”

Neither of them liked what that possibility implied.

Then suddenly—

the lights inside the chamber flickered once.

Only once.

But both of them felt it immediately.

A pulse.

Far away.

Ancient.

Watching.

Kairo found Kaze waiting inside the upper observatory.

The room overlooked the storm-covered skyline of Tokyo through enormous circular windows lined with ancient celestial markings faded by time.

Candles burned quietly around the chamber despite the electric lights remaining functional.

Kaze preferred tradition when discussing dangerous truths.

For several moments, neither father nor son spoke.

Then Kaze finally broke the silence.

“You saw beyond the distortion.”

It was not a question.

Kairo stepped farther into the observatory slowly.

“…What are those things?”

Rain struck the glass softly behind them.

Kaze stared toward the blood-red clouds hanging above the city.

“Fragments.”

Kairo’s expression hardened immediately.

“Fragments of what?”

A long silence followed.

Then—

“Something humanity was never meant to witness.”

The atmosphere inside the observatory felt heavier suddenly.

Older.

As though the room itself remembered things better left forgotten.

Kaze moved toward one of the ancient stone tablets lining the walls of the chamber.

Celestial symbols covered its surface.

Three circles.

Three eyes.

And beneath them—

a black star.

“The world once called it the Silent Heaven,” Kaze said quietly.

“A place beyond human reality where celestial consciousness existed before recorded history.”

Kairo listened carefully.

“The clans believed the Celestial Eyes were blessings sent from that realm.”

Kaze’s expression darkened slightly.

“But blessings and chains often wear the same face.”

Lightning illuminated the observatory briefly.

For a split second, the black star engraved into the stone tablet almost looked alive.

Kairo immediately noticed it.

“…That symbol.”

Kaze closed his eyes slowly.

“Ama-no-Mikaboshi.”

Even speaking the name changed the atmosphere.

The candles flickered violently.

“The Fallen Celestial.”

Silence swallowed the room.

Then Kaze finally looked directly toward Kairo.

“What your generation inherited was never divinity.”

His voice lowered.

“It was containment.”

Kairo stared silently at the ancient tablet.

Containment.

That word refused to leave his mind.

“You’re saying our eyes exist to imprison Mikaboshi?”

Kaze did not answer immediately.

Which terrified him more than any direct response could have.

“The original bloodlines formed after the First Fall,” Kaze said quietly.

“When the black star descended into this world.”

Rain intensified outside the observatory windows.

“The Celestial Eyes were created afterward.”

“To seal it?”

“To divide it.”

Silence.

Kairo’s expression slowly changed.

Understanding.

Horror.

“The Heavenly Eye… the Seraphic Eye…”

“And the Celestial Eye,” Kaze finished softly.

“The Trinity.”

The candles throughout the observatory flickered violently again.

For the first time in years—

Kairo saw genuine fear inside his father’s eyes.

“When the three resonate completely,” Kaze continued, “the fragments begin returning to their original state.”

Kairo’s heartbeat slowed.

“…You mean Mikaboshi reforms.”

Kaze said nothing.

That silence was answer enough.

Lightning illuminated the observatory again.

And suddenly—

Kairo understood why the clan feared Reika so deeply.

Why Kaze watched the twins so carefully.

Why the blood moon mattered.

Why Violet looked at them with pity.

They were not heirs to divinity.

They were pieces of something sleeping.

Then Kairo asked the question he had been avoiding since the beginning.

“…What happens if the Trinity fully awakens?”

The observatory became completely silent.

Even the storm outside seemed to fade for a moment.

Then Kaze answered quietly.

“The Silent Heaven opens.”

And somewhere else inside the estate—

Reika heard a voice whisper her name for the first time.

Reika sat alone inside the eastern wing of the estate.

No lights.

No servants.

No sound beyond the distant rain striking the windows outside.

The room remained almost completely dark except for the faint golden glow leaking through the bandages wrapped around her arms.

The cracks had spread farther.

She could feel them beneath her skin now.

Burning.

Breathing.

Waiting.

Slowly, Reika unwrapped one of the bandages around her wrist.

Golden fractures immediately became visible across her skin like glowing veins carved from light itself.

For a moment—

she almost didn’t recognize her own hand anymore.

A soft knock came from the door.

“Kairo sent food.”

One of the younger servants spoke nervously from outside the room.

Reika closed her eyes.

“…Leave it there.”

The servant hesitated.

Then quickly walked away.

Even they were afraid now.

Reika lowered her head silently.

The worst part was—

she understood why.

Her flames had been seen by the entire city.

People screamed when they looked at her.

The corrupted users didn’t attack Kairo.

They attacked her.

As if the Seraphic Eye itself was calling them.

Then suddenly—

the room temperature dropped.

Reika froze instantly.

The shadows near the corner of the room stretched unnaturally across the floor.

And a voice whispered softly beside her ear.

«“You are opening.”»

Reika’s eye widened.

No one was there.

But she could still feel something breathing beside her in the darkness.

Watching.

Waiting.

Smiling.

Far above the estate—

something stood silently upon the highest rooftop overlooking Tokyo.

Rain passed through its body unnaturally, distorting around the black silhouette instead of touching it directly.

No human eyes could have perceived it.

Not fully.

The figure resembled a man dressed in long dark robes, though parts of its form continuously dissolved into drifting shadow before reconstructing again moments later.

And where its face should have been—

there was only darkness.

It watched the eastern wing of the estate silently.

Watched Reika.

Watched the golden light spreading beneath her skin.

Then slowly—

the darkness beneath its hood shifted.

A single eye opened.

Black.

Ancient.

Endless.

The pressure surrounding the rooftop intensified instantly.

Even the rain began falling differently around the figure.

Like reality itself feared getting too close.

Far below, Reika suddenly felt it.

Her breathing stopped.

The presence from before had returned.

Stronger now.

Closer.

And for a split second—

she saw it reflected faintly in the window beside her.

The Watcher in Black.

Standing behind her.

Reika turned instantly.

Nothing.

The room was empty again.

But the whisper returned one final time.

«“The Silent Heaven remembers you.”»

Then the golden cracks across her body spread farther.

The rain had not stopped.

Night wrapped itself tightly around the estate as silence settled across the halls once again.

Reika stood alone on one of the eastern balconies overlooking the distant lights of Tokyo.

The city seemed smaller now.

Fragile.

As if one wrong decision could break everything.

“You always come here when you're thinking.”

Reika didn't turn around.

She already recognized the voice.

Kairo stepped onto the balcony beside her.

For several moments neither twin spoke.

The rain beyond the roofline filled the silence for them.

Finally, Kairo leaned against the railing.

“You didn't eat.”

Reika rolled her eyes slightly.

“That's what you came here to say?”

“No.”

A faint smile appeared briefly.

Then vanished.

The seriousness returned.

Kairo looked toward the city.

“I spoke with Father.”

Reika immediately understood.

The truth.

Or at least part of it.

“The Silent Heaven?”

Kairo nodded.

The wind shifted gently around them.

For a while neither knew what to say.

Because once certain truths were spoken—

life never returned to normal.

Eventually Reika broke the silence.

“Do you ever wish we were ordinary?”

Kairo blinked.

That was not a question she asked lightly.

The golden cracks beneath her sleeve pulsed faintly.

Almost unnoticed.

Almost.

“Sometimes,” Kairo admitted.

Reika laughed softly.

Not because it was funny.

Because it hurt.

“I used to think our eyes made us special.”

The smile disappeared.

“Now I think they're making us disappear.”

Kairo turned toward her immediately.

“Reika—”

“I heard something tonight.”

Silence.

The words hung in the air.

Dangerous.

Kairo's expression hardened.

“What did you hear?”

Reika looked away.

Toward the city.

Toward the rain.

Toward anything except him.

Because if she looked directly at her brother—

she might tell him everything.

The whisper.

The darkness.

The voice.

Instead she forced a small smile.

“Probably nothing.”

Kairo knew she was lying.

Reika knew he knew.

Neither pushed further.

For now.

Above them, hidden beyond the clouds and rain—

something watched silently.

Waiting.

Patient.

Ancient.Far beneath Tokyo—

deeper than the subway tunnels.

Deeper than the foundations of the city itself.

A hidden chamber remained untouched by time.

Ancient stone walls covered in celestial symbols surrounded a massive circular structure embedded into the earth.

The seal.

One of the oldest remaining restraints placed upon Ama-no-Mikaboshi after the First Fall.

Cracks now spread across its surface.

Slowly.

Inevitably.

A single figure stood before it.

Zenith.

The former elder watched the fractures silently while black fragments drifted around him like living shadows.

He looked neither pleased nor concerned.

Only certain.

Behind him, several Veilbreakers waited nervously.

One finally gathered enough courage to speak.

“The distortions are accelerating faster than expected.”

Zenith remained silent.

“The Seraphic Eye awakened publicly.”

Still silent.

“The clan is mobilizing.”

A faint smile crossed Zenith’s face.

“Good.”

The Veilbreakers exchanged uneasy glances.

Zenith slowly raised his hand toward the fractured seal.

The black star engraved at its center pulsed faintly.

Like a heartbeat.

“For centuries,” Zenith said quietly, “they called it containment.”

His fractured Abyss Eye opened.

“But cages eventually become tombs.”

The chamber trembled.

Dust fell from the ceiling.

The cracks spread farther.

Something ancient stirred beyond the seal.

And for the first time in many years—

Zenith bowed his head.

Not in fear.

In reverence.

«“The heavens are opening.”»

The pulse that followed spread outward beneath Tokyo.

And somewhere inside the Tenshirō estate—

Reika collapsed to her knees.

Reika hit the floor hard.

The pain arrived without warning.

One moment she had been standing.

The next—

every nerve in her body felt like it was burning.

The golden cracks beneath her skin ignited with blinding light.

Servants nearby shouted in alarm.

Footsteps rushed toward her.

But their voices sounded distant.

Muted.

As though she were sinking beneath deep water.

“Reika!”

Kairo's voice.

Far away.

The world around her dissolved.

Darkness swallowed everything.

Then—

silence.

No pain.

No sound.

No sky.

No ground.

Only endless blackness stretching in every direction.

Reika slowly rose to her feet.

“Where am I...?”

Her voice echoed strangely.

The darkness responded.

Not with words.

With presence.

Ancient.

Immense.

Watching.

Then she saw it.

Far away—

a single black star hanging within the void.

Its light felt wrong.

Older than time.

Older than memory.

And beneath it stood a silhouette.

Humanoid.

Motionless.

Waiting.

Reika's heartbeat accelerated.

The figure slowly lifted its head.

Two burning eyes opened.

And a voice filled the darkness.

Not spoken.

Felt.

«“At last.”»

The void trembled.

The black star pulsed once.

«“I have found you.”»

Reika froze.

The presence felt familiar.

Terrifyingly familiar.

As though part of her had always known it.

The voice spoke again.

«“Little Seraph.”»

«“Do you remember me?”»

Then the darkness shattered.

Reika's eyes opened violently.

She gasped for breath.

Back inside the estate.

Back in reality.

Kairo knelt beside her.

Fear visible on his face.

For several seconds she could not speak.

Could not move.

Could only stare upward.

Because she now knew one thing with absolute certainty.

Something inside the darkness knew her name.
